11:T8be,<h2>Green Desert participates in Lithuanian hemp conference supporting “Kanapės 2035”</h2>
<p>Green Desert participated in a major <strong>industrial hemp conference</strong> held in Lithuania, which brought together policymakers, industry leaders, researchers, and international companies active across the hemp value chain.</p>
<p>The event focused on the proposal of a national development strategy titled <strong><em>Kanapės 2035</em></strong>, aiming to significantly scale Lithuania’s industrial hemp sector, including a potential <strong>100-fold increase in fiber hemp cultivation</strong>. Discussions covered regulatory frameworks, processing infrastructure, innovation, and the role of hemp in the European bioeconomy.</p>
<p>Green Desert was represented by its co-founder <strong>Abdulhadi Al-Amer</strong>, who took part as an international industry stakeholder. During the conference, Green Desert shared its perspective as a <strong>MENA-based biotech company</strong> developing hemp-based solutions for construction, agriculture, and industrial applications.</p>
<p>The participation highlighted Green Desert’s interest in <strong>cross-regional collaboration</strong> between Europe and the Middle East, particularly around sustainable materials, knowledge transfer, and scalable hemp value chains. These discussions align with the company’s broader mission to support climate-positive industrial solutions and contribute to long-term sustainability strategies such as <strong>Saudi Vision 2030</strong>.</p>
<p>By engaging in international forums like this conference, Green Desert continues to position itself as an <strong>active contributor to the global industrial hemp ecosystem</strong>, supporting policy dialogue, innovation, and responsible market development.</p>
